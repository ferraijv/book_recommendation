Solarpunk is the subgenre of climate fiction that focuses on repair: neighborhoods powered by clean energy, citizens rebuilding mutual aid, and imaginative solutions that feel tangible. Use this starter shelf as a guided path—begin with the gentle reads and move into the bigger, system-sized novels when you’re ready to go wide.

<div class="community-box">
  <strong>Reading tip:</strong> Pair one story-driven novel with one idea-rich title to keep both your heart and curiosity engaged. Switch formats (novel, novella, anthology) to avoid climate-fatigue and keep momentum.
</div>

## Solarpunk FAQ
<div class="community-box">
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Is solarpunk only science fiction?</strong> It leans sci-fi, but you’ll also find cozy, literary, and speculative takes that emphasize community and ecological balance.</li>
    <li><strong>Do I need to read anthologies first?</strong> Not required—anthologies like <em>Sunvault</em> are great for sampling the vibe, but novels are just as welcoming.</li>
    <li><strong>What if I want something more intense?</strong> Try the contrast read (<em>The Water Knife</em>) to feel the stakes before returning to hopeful futures.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## Build-Your-Own Solarpunk TBR
<div class="community-box">
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Start with a cozy entry:</strong> Try <em>A Psalm for the Wild-Built</em> if you want compact, healing narratives.</li>
    <li><strong>Zoom out for policy &amp; systems:</strong> <em>The Ministry for the Future</em> blends fiction with policy ideas.</li>
    <li><strong>Pair with movement history:</strong> <em>The Fifth Sacred Thing</em> shows how community values scale.</li>
    <li><strong>Mix hope with caution:</strong> Add <em>The Water Knife</em> for a sobering “what if we don’t act” read.</li>
  </ul>
